Tibok-tibok Recipe






Tibok-tibok or carabao-milk pudding is a Pampangan dessert or snack pudding made primarily from carabao milk and ground soaked glutinous rice. Originating in the Philippine province of Pampanga, it is especially popular in Cagayan. It has a soft jelly-like texture and is topped with latik before serving.



Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 2 hrs 40 mins



Ingredients:


  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 4 cups fresh carabao’s milk (or cow’s milk)
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (if using cow’s milk)


Instructions:


  1. 1. In a pan over medium heat, add coconut cream and bring to a boil. Continue to cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid starts to thicken.
  2. 2. Lower heat and simmer. As oil starts to separate and solids begin to form, regularly stir and scrape sides and bottom of the pan to prevent from burning. Continue to cook and stir until curds turn golden brown.
  3. 3. Using a fine mesh sieve or colander, drain latik. Reserve oil.
  4. 4. Generously brush bottom and sides of 7 x 5-inch pan with coconut oil and set aside.
  5. 5. In a heavy-bottomed pot, combine milk, rice flour, and sugar. Add salt if using cow's milk. Whisk together until smooth and well-blended..
  6. 6. Over medium-low heat, bring to a simmer, whisking regularly. Continue to cook, whisking regularly, for about 10 to 15 minutes or until mixture thickens to a smooth thick paste.
  7. 7. Gently transfer milk mixture to prepared pan. Smooth and evenly distribute using a spatula.
  8. 8. Allow to slightly cool and set. Generously brush with coconut oil and garnish with latik. Cut into serving slices.



Banana Bread Recipe






It became very popular due to the recipe’s simplicity. In the 1930s, it was a regular feature in American cookbooks, right when baking powder and baking soda became popular. Though this recipe originated in a foreign country, it’s become one of the most favorite Filipino foods! You can even say that the Filipino people have adopted this recipe and have been enjoying it for decades.



Preparation Time: 5 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour 10 minutes



Ingredients:


  • 3 ripe bananas mashed
  • 2 eggs
  • 1¾ cups flour
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ¼ cup evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract


Instructions:


  1. 1. Preheat the oven to 325 F.
  2. 2. Combine the eggs, oil, sugar, milk, vanilla, and mashed bananas either by hand, or with a hand mixer or stand mixer.
  3. 3. Add the baking soda and flour.
  4. 4. Combine and mix well.
  5. 5. Pour into a greased loaf pan and bake for 1 hour and 10 minutes.
  6. 6. Slice and serve.



Chocolate Crinkles Recipe






Chocolate Crinkles are soft and fudgy chocolate cookies enclosed in confectioners or powdered sugar. A perfect holiday treat these delightful cookies (baked with lots of love) are good christmas presents for friends or even a luscious christmas dessert to share with the entire family.



Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 28 minutes



Ingredients:


  • 2 cups all-purpose flour sifted
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • 1 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 pieces eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 5 ounces bitter sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 cup powdered sugar


Instructions:


  1. 1. Melt Chocolate chip using a double boiler or chocolate melter.
  2. 2. Combine flour, salt, and baking powder in a large bowl. Mix well. Set aside.
  3. 3. Combine sugar and cocoa powder in a mixing bowl. Mix alltogether until well distributed.Continue mixing while adding oil, vanilla extract, melted chocolate chips, and eggs. Note: Using a stand mixer with paddle attachment is ideal for this task.
  4. 4. Gradually add the flour mixture while mixing. Continue the process until all the ingredients are well distributed. Cover the mixing bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  5. 5. Preheat oven to 350F. Scoop a tablespoon of chocolate mixture and shape it into a ball using your hands. Roll it over the powdered sugar until completely coated. Arrange in a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Perform this step until the chocolate mixture is completely consumed.
  6. 6. Bake for 13 minutes.
  7. 7. Remove from the oven and let it cool for 10 minutes.
    8. 8. Serve. Share and enjoy!


    Suman Pinipig Recipe






    It is made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, often wrapped in banana leaves, coconut leaves, or buli or buri palm (Corypha) leaves for steaming. It is usually eaten sprinkled with sugar or laden with latik. A widespread variant of suman uses cassava instead of glutinous rice



    Preparation Time: 10 minutes

    Cooking Time: 30 minutes



    Ingredients:


    • 1 ½ cup pinipig
    • 2 cups coconut milk
    • ¾ cup sugar
    • ¼ tsp salt
    • ¼ tsp pandan extract


    Instructions:


    1. 1. In a pan over medium heat, add coconut milk and bring to a boil. Stirring occasionally until liquid starts to thicken.
    2. 2. Lower heat and let simmer. When oil starts to separate and solids begin to form, stir and scrape sides and bottom of the pan to prevent from burning the curd. Continue to cook and stir until curds turn golden brown. Transfer in a bowl.
    3. 3. Prepare banana leaves approximately 6 inches by 8 inches by wiping with clean paper towels then passing it on stove top flames to make it soft and pliable. Set aside.
    4. 4. Heat medium pan over medium heat and tip in the pinipig for 2-4 minutes. Remove and transfer pinipig in a bowl. Set aside
    5. 5 Using the same pan, pour coconut milk, sugar and salt. Stir until granules dissolve and cover with a lid. Simmer for 3-5 minutes over medium heat. Add pandan extract and stir until uniform in color.
    6. 6. Tip in toasted pinipig and stir-cook until pinipig absorbs the coconut milk and the consistency is thick, cohesive an does not stick to the pan. Remove from heat and let it cool down.
    7. 7. Lay banana leaf in a flat surface then scoop 1 ½ tbsp. of the pinipig mixture near the edge of the banana leaf. Mold the pinipig mix into a cylindrical shape approx. 4-5 inches long and spread 1 to 2 tsp of latik on top.
    8. 8. Roll the banana leaf firmly to wrap the pinipig mix then fold both sides of the leaf to secure the mixture inside. Repeat the process until all pinipig mixture is done. Serve and enjoy. Bon appetit!



    Kamote Que Recipe






    Kamote Que is deep fried sweet potatoes with a coating of caramelized brown sugar. This is a common meryenda (mid-afternoon snack) in the Philippines and can mostly be seen peddled around the busy streets. I would say that Kamote Que is one of the top selling meryenda because of its affordability and filling effect (it can make you feel full for several hours). This can also recharge your energy temporarily (carb and sugar combined makes you hyper active).



    Preparation Time: 10 minutes

    Cooking Time: 10 minutes



    Ingredients:


    • 1 lb sweet potato sliced crosswise about 1.5 cm thick (Asian sweet potato is preferred)
    • 1 cup brown sugar
    • 1 cup cooking oil


    Instructions:


    1. 1. Heat the pan and pour the cooking oil.
    2. 2. Let the cooking oil heat-up then put-in the brown sugar.
    3. 3. When the brown sugar floats, put-in the sweet potato and fry for 7 to 10 minutes. Flip the sweet potato once in a while to allow the sugar to stick.
    4. 4. Remove the sweet potato from the pan one after the other. You could directly skewer the fried sweet potatoes once removed from the pan.
    5. 5. Let it cool down for a few minutes then serve. Share and Enjoy.



    Bananacue Recipe






    Banana cue or bananacue is a popular snack food or street food in the Philippines. It is a portmanteau of banana and barbecue, which in Philippine English refers to meat cooked in a style similar to satay.



    Preparation Time: 5 minutes

    Cooking Time: 15 minutes



    Ingredients:


    • 6 pieces saging na saba Asian plantains
    • 2 cups brown sugar
    • 4 cups cooking oil


    Instructions:


    1. 1. Heat a cooking pot then pour-in cooking oil.
    2. 2. When the oil becomes hot, deep fry the bananas for 2 minutes.
    3. 3. Gradually put-in the brown sugar, adjust the heat to medium-low and continue cooking until the melted brown sugar coats the bananas. Note: Gently stir the bananas so that it can get coated with melted brown sugar easily.
    4. 4. Remove the cooked bananas one by one and immediately skewer using a bamboo skewer. Note: 2 pieces per skewer is recommended.
    5. 5. Let cool then serve with cold soda.
    6. 6. Share and enjoy!



    Biko With Latik Recipe






    Some of the primary characteristics that enter our minds when we think of cake are probably light, fluffy and sugary sweet. But veering off to the East might introduce us to another kind of cake altoge



    Preparation Time: 5 minutes

    Cooking Time: 50 minutes



    Ingredients:


    • 2 cups glutinous rice washed
    • 1 ½ cups water
    • 2 cups brown sugar
    • 4 cups coconut milk
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 2 cups coconut milk


    Instructions:


    1. 1. Prepare latik by boiling 2 cups coconut milk in a saucepan. Adjust heat to medium. Continue boiling while stirring until texture thickens. Lower the heat once oil becomes visible. Cook and stir constantly until the color of the residue turns golden brown (note: latik is the residue formed after boiling coconut milk). Separate the oil from the latik using a strainer or filter paper. Set aside.
    2. 2. Start making the biko by combining glutinous rice and water in a rice cooker. Set the cooker to “cook” mode. Wait until the process is complete.
    3. 3. Boil 4 cups coconut milk in a cooking pot for 7 minutes.
    4. 4. Add brown sugar and salt. Stir.
    5. 5. Add cooked rice. Stir until well blended. Continue cooking while stirring until rice absorbs the coconut milk mixture completely.
    6. 6. Line banana leaves over a baking tray. Brush coconut oil over the leaves.
    7. 7. Transfer biko into the tray once the consistency becomes thick and sticky (paste-like). Top with latik. Let it cool down for at least 15 minutes.
      8. 8. Serve. Share and enjoy!


      Maja Blanca Recipe






      Maja Blanca is a Filipino dessert made from coconut milk, cornstarch, and sugar. Often called Coconut Pudding, this luscious dessert is easy to make and the ingredients are very common.



      Preparation Time: 8 minutes

      Cooking Time: 43 minutes



      Ingredients:


      • 4 cups coconut milk
      • 3/4 cup cornstarch
      • 14 ounces condensed milk
      • 3/4 cup fresh milk
      • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
      • 15 ounces whole sweet kernel corn
      • 5 tbsp toasted grated coconut


      Instructions:


      1. 1. Pour the coconut milk in a cooking pot and bring to a boil.
      2. 2. Add the sugar, condensed milk, and whole sweet kernel corn then stir until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.
      3. 3. Simmer for 8 minutes
      4. 4. Combine the milk and cornstarch then whisk until the cornstarch is diluted
      5. 5. Pour the fresh milk and cornstarch mixture in the cooking pot and stir thoroughly.
      6. 6. Allow to cook while stirring until the mixture reaches your desired thickness
      7. 7. Pour the mixture in a serving tray then arrange and flatten the top using a flat tool such as a wooden spatula
      8. 8. Allow to cool down then refrigerate for at least 1 hour
      9. 9. Garnish with toasted grated coconut (or latik if available)
      10. 10. Serve cold. Share and enjoy!



      Turon Recipe






      Turon is a type of Filipino snack. It is also called deep-fried banana rolls or banana lumpia. A typical Filipino meryenda and dessert, Turon has gained popularity throughout the years. This sweet snack can usually be seen on the streets being peddled along with bananacue or even vegetable lumpia.



      Preparation Time: 10 minutes

      Cooking Time: 22 minutes



      Ingredients:


      • 6 pieces bananas saba or plantains, cut in half (lengthwise)
      • 1 cup jackfruit ripe, sliced
      • 1 1/2 cup sugar
      • 12 pieces lumpia wrapper
      • 2 cups cooking oil


      Instructions:


      1. 1. Roll the banana on the sugar plate and ensure that the banana is coated with enough sugar
      2. 2. Place the banana with sugar coating in the spring roll wrapper and add some ripe jackfruit
      3. 3. Fold and lock the spring roll wrapper, use water to seal the edge
      4. 4. In a pan, heat the oil and put-in some sugar.Wait until the brown sugar floats
      5. 5. Put-in the wrapped banana and fry until the wrapper turns golden brown and the extra sugar sticks on wrapper
      6. 6. Serve hot as a dessert or snack. Share and Enjoy!



      Puto Recipe






      Puto is traditionally made of rice. The grains are soaked overnight in water to soften and then processed into a soft dough called galapong. The natural fermentation of soaking the rice acts as a leavening agent, but it's also common to add yeast or baking powder to help the cake rise



      Preparation Time: 5 minutes

      Cooking Time: 35 minutes



      Ingredients:


      • 1 cup all-purpose flour
      • 1/2 cup granulated white sugar
      • 3 teaspoons baking powder
      • 1 cup fresh milk
      • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
      • 3/4 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
      • 5 cups water for steaming


      Instructions:


      1. 1. Pour the water into a steamer or cooking pot. Let boil.
      2. 2. Sift flour, sugar, and baking powder. Combine in a bowl. Mix well.
      3. 3. Gradually add the milk while stirring. Continue to stir until the texture of the mixture becomes smooth.
      4. 4. Stir-in the vanilla extract and half of the cheese.
      5. 5. Pour the mixture into small to medium sized molds. Arrange in a steamer. Cover and steam for 25 minutes. Top each puto with the remaining cheese. Steam for 5 minutes more.
      6. 6. Take the steamed cheese puto out of the steamer. Let it cool down.
      7. 7. Gently remove the puto from the mold with the help of a spatula.
      8. 8. Arrange in serving plate. Serve.
      9. 9. Share and enjoy!
